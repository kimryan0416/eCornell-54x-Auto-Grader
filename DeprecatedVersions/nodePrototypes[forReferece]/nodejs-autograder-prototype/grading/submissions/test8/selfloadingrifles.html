Self-Loading Rifles

Historical Use of Self-Loading Rifles

A self-loading rifle is a weapon that is capable of semi-automatic or automatic fire. While most people think of the M1 Garand when thinking of semi-automatic rifles, many prototype semi-automatic rifles were produced by all nations in WWI. Though a few were adopted for regular service, most would not see full scale production. Although the weapons would have been better at close quarters than regular bolt-action rifles, many had trouble with reliability in the muddy environments of trenches. This combined with their higher cost ensured that they would never replace bolt-action rifles in WWI.

In-Game

Self-Loading Rifles excel at medium to long range, so keep your distance and pick off enemies one by one. Knowing when to engage and when to back off is key to succeeding with a self-loading rifle.

Recommendations

For long range, the M1916 is my choice as it has a high ammo count, making follow up shots easy, good accuracy, and good damage. For short range, the Autoloading 8 Extended variant offers the best combination of ammo capacity, fire rate, and damage.

Cei-Rigotti

The Cei-Rigotti is the starting self-loading rifle in Battlefield, and like most starting weapons it acts as a jack of all trades type of weapon. It comes with a generous 10 round capacity, which makes the weapon very forgiving if you miss a shot. It also has a very good bullet velocity, which means hitting moving targets requires less of a lead. The Cei-Rigotti can also has a fully automatic mode, allowing you to compete with submachine guns in a pinch. However, the weapon has less than average damage to balance it out. It comes in three variants:

  • Factory: This variant recovers from recoil faster and has less recoil overall.
  • Optical: This variant has a reflex sight for long range engagements.
  • Trench: This variant has better hipfire accuracy, which means its better at close range.

Designed by Colonel Amerigo Cei-Rigotti in the early 1900's, the Cei-Rigotti was Italy's first attempt at a self-loading rifle. It was meant to use parts from the Carcano, Italy's standard bolt action rifle, but its final design had little commonality with the Carcano. Although the rifle was tested by the British and Italian armies for adoption, the Cei-Rigotti was ultimately rejected for full scale production.

Selbstlader M1916

The Selbstlader M1916 is a well balanced self-loading rifle best used at long range. Although the 25 round magazine would make one think that this weapon is for close range, the weapon is hampered by a slow rate of fire, allowing submachine guns and shotguns to rip you apart before you get your second shot off. To compensate, the M1916 has good damage and a fast muzzle velocity of 800m/s to dominate long range fights. The Selbstlader comes in three variants:

  • Factory: This variant recovers from recoil faster and has less recoil overall.
  • Optical: This variant has a reflex sight for long range engagements.
  • Marksman: This variant has a medium powered scope for very long range engagements.

The Selbstlader-Karabiner M1916, was the last gun in a series of semi-automatic rifles designed by German arms manufacturer Mauser. Although the rifle was very accurate, its delicate internal mechanisms were very sensitive to dirt. The rifle would then be adopted by the German Air Force, where sensitivity to dirt was generally less of an issue. However, the high cost of the weapon and the greater number of machine guns available for planes meant that only a small number of weapons would be built.

M1907 SL

The M1907 SL is a close range self-loading rifle. It features a 20 round magazine and very good damage, meaning that it can kill a lot of enemies in quick succession. However, high recoil and a low bullet velocity mean that the M1907 SL will struggle to hit anything not in close range. The M1907 SL comes in three variants:

  • Factory: This variant recovers from recoil faster and has less recoil overall.
  • Trench: This variant has better hipfire accuracy, which means its better at close range.
  • Sweeper: This variant adds a fully automatic fire mode, allowing you kill enemies faster in very close range.

The M1907 SL is based on the Winchester Model 1907, a semi-automatic rifle that was originally designed as a hunting rifle. The weapon would be purchased in large quantities after the outbreak in WWI by France, Great Britain, Russia, and the US. They were originally used by gunners in aircraft, but as aircraft machine guns became more commonplace they were transferred to use by infantry. The French especially used it as an assault weapon, adopting a fully automatic version in 1917. The rifle would continued to be produced after the war, especially by law enforcement.

Mondragon

The Mondrogan is a self-loading rifle that is best used at long range. It does good damage, and importantly the damage does not decrease as much as longer ranges compared to other rifles. This combined with a muzzle velocity of 800m/s makes dropping targets at long ranges easy. However, it has a slow rate of fire making it incapable of competing at close range. It comes in three variants:

  • Storm: This variant has less recoil, a good all around choice.
  • Optical: This variant has a reflex sight for medium to long range engagements.
  • Sniper: This variant has a high power scope and a bipod for very long range.

Autoloading 8

The Autoloading 8 is a unique self-loading rifle. It has a very high rate of fire, but only has 5 rounds. As three of these rounds must land to kill a full health enemy, the rifle demands a high degree of accuracy from its user. The Autoloading 8 is good for small engagements where a player only faces one to two enemies, as reloads are frequent with this gun. However, the range of the engagement is mostly irrelevant as the Autoloading 8 is good at close and long range. The Autoloading 8 has three variants:

  • Factory: This variant recovers from recoil faster and has less recoil overall.
  • Marksman: This variant has a medium powered scope for long range engagements.
  • Extended: This variant has a 15 round magazine but reduces damage, making it more forgiving but making it less powerful against one opponent.

RSC 1917

The RSC is a weapon that is best used at medium to long range. Its most defining feature is its extremely high damage. It is capable of a two shot kill at close range and a three shot kill at long range, more than any other self-loading rifle. The RSC also has a decent bullet velocity of 720m/s, and average recoil, so good players should be able to land shots on distant enemies. The RSC has two variants:

  • Factory: This variant recovers from recoil faster and has less recoil overall.
  • Optical: This variant has a reflex sight for medium to long range engagements.

The RSC M1917, also know as the Fusil Automatique Mod√®le 1917, was a French semi-automatic rifle adopted into service in 1917. Sharing the same cartridge as the Lebel, the standard bolt action rifle of France, and many of the same parts, the RSC was meant to be an easy to manufacture semi-automatic rifle to supplement the Lebel. 86,000 rifles were manufactured, making it one of the first semi-automatic rifles issued on a large scale. The rifle was largely forgotten after WWI, only used in small numbers in colonial campaigns and WWII.
