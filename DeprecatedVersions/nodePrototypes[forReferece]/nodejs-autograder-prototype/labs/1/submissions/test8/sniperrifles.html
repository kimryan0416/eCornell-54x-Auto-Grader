Sniper Rifles

Historical Use of Sniper Rifles

Although snipers had existed before, World War 1 was the first use of scoped sniper rifles on a large scale. At the start of the war, only Imperial Germany possessed scoped rifles due to their ability to manufacture high quality lenses. However, the French and British quickly started training their own snipers in 1915 and equipping them with scopes as well. Snipers either used a scope attached directly to their rifles or used a seperate periscope to stay concealed in a trench.

In-Game

Used by the Scout class, these rifles have long range and good accuracy to eliminate targets from afar. Players have the option to equip a scope or use the iron sights. Rifles also have a sweet spot, or a range where the rifle will kill in one hit to the body, incentivizing players to stay within this range.

Recommendations

For long range, the Gewehr 98 is probably the best as its bullet velocity makes long range shooting incredibly easy. For a more objective based sniper, the SMLE Mk. III has an ideal sweet spot and high capacity, making it a good choice. The Martini-Henry is a good choice for aggressive players with very good aim.

Lebel 1886

The Lebel is a sniper rifle that is in the middle of other rifles like the MKIII or the Springfield. It's one shot kill ranges from 50m to 87m, which allows for playing near the objective while still being at a safe distance. It also has a capacity of 8 rounds, and has a relatively high rate of fire as well, allowing you to take down multiple enemies quickly. Its greatest weakness is its extremely long reload, so be sure to find a safe spot before trying. It has two variants:

  • Infantry: This variant uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil quicker, making it better for close range.
  • Sniper: This variant comes with a high powered scope and a bipod, making it better at very long range.

Introduced as the standard infantry rifle of France in 1886, the Lebel was revolutionary because it was the first standard rifle to use smokeless power. It also held 8 rounds in an underbarrel tublar magazine, giving it greater capacity than any other rifle of the day. Its adoption started an arms race with Germany, which was forced to adopt a new rifle to counter the Lebel. The French had planned to replace the Lebel in 1913, but the threat of war forced it stop and stick with the more numerous Lebel. In combat, the old tubular magazine proved to be the rifle's greatest weakness. Although the Lebel had a high capacity, it was very awkward and slow to reload in combat as it could not use a stripper clip like other rifles of the day. The rifle was ultimately replaced after the war by the MAS-36, though shortages meant that the Lebel would still see service even in WWII.

SMLE Mk. III

The SMLE Mk. III is the sniper rifle for players who want to be aggressive. It's sweet spot is goes from 40-75 meters, one of the closest in-game. This incentivizes players to stay close to objectives rather than camp halfway across the map. The SMLE is unique in that holds 10 rounds, the most of any sniper rifle, allowing you to focus on shooting and not on your ammo count. This combined with the fact that non-scoped variants can fire continuously when aimed down sights makes the SMLE fearsome at close to medium range. The SMLE comes in three variants:

  • Infantry: This variants uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil faster.
  • Marksman: This variant comes with a medium powered scope for long range sniping.
  • Carbine: This variant adds a reflex sight and is more accurate when moving for medium range shooting.

The SMLE Mk III, standing for Short Magazine Lee-Enfield, was the most recent version of the Lee-Enfield rifle when World War One broke out. It quickly gained a reputation for being a fearsome battle rifle capable of laying unending volleys of fire thanks too its 10-round magazine. It was also very fast firing, and British soldiers were training to perform the mad minute, where 20-30 rounds were firing in 60 seconds. There are even stories of German soldiers mistaking the fire from Lee-Enfields with that of machine guns. The Lee-Enfield would go on to serve in WWII and beyond, though it was modified again as the SMLE No. 4 Mk I.

Gewehr 98

The Gewehr 98 is one of two bolt action rifles in the game that specializes in long range combat. Its sweet spot ranges from 80-125m, the second furthest of all sniper rifles. It's defining feature is its bullet velocity of 880m/s, which is the highest in the whole game. This bullet velocity makes shooting at long ranges very easy, as leading the target becomes almost unnecessary. However, the Gewehr also has a slow rate of fire which could allow enemies to kill you as you cycle the bolt, so be sure to be in cover between shots. The Gewehr 98 comes in three variants:

  • Infantry: This variants uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil faster.
  • Marksman: This variant comes with a medium powered scope for long range sniping.
  • Sniper: This variant comes with a high powered scope and a bipod, making it better at very long range.

Designed by the German arms manufacturer Mauser, the Gewehr 98 is one of the most famous rifles ever made. This is mostly because of the action of the rifle, which allows to gun to fire high powered rounds safely, made the rifle extremely accurate, and were very durable. This part of the gun has been copied in many other weapons, like the American Springfield and the Japanese Arisaka, and even in modern bolt action rifles. It was replaced in WWII by the Karabiner 98k, a shortened version of the Gewehr 98. However, the original rifle still sees some use as a hunting rifle.

Gewehr M.95

The Gewehr M.95 is unique amongst the sniper rifles in that it does not have a sweet spot, meaning it is impossible to get a one shot kill to the body. It also has as slow bullet velocity of only 620m/s. While this may make the weapon sound useless, the M.95 has two advantages to make up for this. The first is its incredibly high rate of fire of 67 rounds per minute, one of the highest of all the sniper rifles. The second is the fact that the gun can be fired continuously when aiming down sights for all variants, allowing you to keep track of your target if you miss. These advantages make the M.95 a forgiving close quarters weapon. The M.95 also has a quick reload that does not change depending on how many bullets you have left, meaning that you can quickly reload after killing an enemy and be ready for the next. The M.95 has three variants:

  • Infantry: This variants uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil faster.
  • Marksman: This variant comes with a medium powered scope for medium range sniping.
  • Carbine: This variant adds a reflex sight, is more accurate when moving, and has better hipfire accuracy.

The M1895 was an Austro-Hungarian weapon designed by Ferdinand Ritter von Mannlicher. It was unique due to its straight pull bolt, which would be operated faster that the standard bolts used by virtually ever other rifle in the war. However the straight pull bolt required more care than a regular bolt due to its mechanical complexity. It also used an en-bloc clip rather than a stripper clip. This meant that the clip and all five rounds had to be inserted at once, with no option to load rounds one at a time. After the war and collapse of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, most of the rifles were sold to the Balkan states, where they would see use in WWII.

Russian 1895

The Russian 1895 is a sniper rifle best used in medium range. It has a sweet spot which ranges from 60-100m, putting it in the middle of the pack. It also has a fast fire rate with its lever action, though not as fast as the M.95. The rifle also has a fast bullet velocity of 820m/s making long range shooting easier. However, the reload of the 1895 is pretty sluggish, so make sure that you find cover before doing so. The Russian 1895 comes in three variants:

  • Infantry: This variant uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil faster.
  • Sniper: This variant comes with a high powered scope and a bipod, making it better at very long range.
  • Trench: This variant has no sweet spot, but increases rate of fire and hipfire accuracy to make it a better close quarters weapon similar to the M.95.

The Winchester 1895 was a lever action rifle and a descendant of the famous Winchester Model 1873, the gun associated with the wild west. It was the first Winchester rifle that used an internal box magazine that could be reloaded with stripper clips, as opposed to the tube magazine of older Winchester rifles. The Russian Empire, short on weapons to equip their vast armies, purchased 300,000 rifles and converted them to Russian calibers. There would account for 70% of all 1895's produced, the rest sold to the US Army or as hunting rifles.

M1903 Springfield

The M1903 is the sniper rifle used for extreme long range shooting. It has the furthest sweet spot of any rifle with it ranging from 100-150m, a very generous distance. It also has a very fast muzzle velocity of 820m/s, making leading those long range shots a little easier. The M1903 is also unique in that it also suffers from less bullet drag, which means that bullets will maintain their velocity to a further range than any other gun, further aiding with long range shooting. The M1903 comes in three different variants:

  • Experimental: This variant changes the M1903 into a semiautomatic rifle with reduced damage but a high rate of fire.
  • Marksman: This variant comes with a medium powered scope for medium to long range sniping.
  • Sniper: This variant comes with a high powered scope and a bipod, making it better at very long range.

The M1903 Springfield was created in response to the Spanish-American War, when the US realized that their Krag-Jorgenson rifle was severly outgunned. Studying the M1893 Mausers that gave them so much trouble, they developed the M1903 Springfield that borrowed from the Mauser design and the old Krag Jorgenson design. It was the US Army's main service rifle in WWI, and would continue to see action in WWII as a sniper rifle and as the service rifle of the Marine Corps. It would continue to serve as a sniper well into the Vietnam War.

Martini-Henry

The Martini-Henry has several unique features, which make it one of the strangest sniper rifles in game. This most noticable feature is its capacity; it only holds one round and must reload after firing it. This makes good aim extremely important. In addition, the muzzle velocity is the slowest of all sniper rifles at a paltry 440m/s, requiring one to lead even medium range targets. However, these flaws are balanced out by the Martini-Henry's defining characteristic: its damage. From 42-68m from your target it will do a 112 damage, meaning that even hitting the arms or legs of your target will result in an instant kill. From 68-80m, it will perform like a normal sniper rifle sweet spot. Because of these feature, the Martini-Henry should be used at close range to take advantage of the enormous damage, but is very unforgiving if a shot is missed. The Martini-Henry has two variants:

  • Infantry: This variant uses the iron sights and recovers from recoil faster.
  • Sniper: This variant comes with a high powered scope and a bipod, making it better at very long range.

Entering service in 1871, the Martini-Henry was the rifle commonly associated with late British imperialism. It served in the First Boer War, the Second Anglo-Afghan War, and most famously in the Anglo-Zulu War where it features in movies like Zulu from 1964. As it came before the 1886 Lebel, it still used black powder to propel its massive .577 caliber round. Though its heyday had passed by WWI, it was still used in the African and Middle Eastern fronts. Amazing, some working rifles have been found by US soldiers as late as 2010.
