About the Apple Festival

For Ithaca, NY, the Apple Festival is a big deal. Local businesses, varying from farmers to restaurants, come together for this apple crazed camaraderie. These apples are from the second largest apple producers in the country. New York is home to over 650 growers on an impressive amount of land. The apple fields stretch over 47,000 acres and produce over 30 million bushels of apple. From 2011 to 2015, New York has witnessed a 750% increase in hard cider production and a 1200% growth in cider producers! Most of the festival's food vendors will be on the 100 block of South Cayuga and the 100 block of West State Street. Amongst them, there will undoubtedly be an insane line for the apple donuts! Witness the love, hard work and joy at the Apple Festival. A great time guaranteed. [1]

Admittance is Free

Vendors will be selling food, merchandise, and more at the Apple Festival!

Parking

Parking is available in the Commons. There are parking garages located on Green St., Seneca St., and Cayuga St. for $5.

A staple of the Fall calendar."
